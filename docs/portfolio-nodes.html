Product Design for Nodes & Links

Intro

Nodes & Links is a technology company that works with global leaders in the project delivery space, from energy and construction to aerospace and defence. Their AI platform help teams access critical project knowledge fast and easy. As a result, projects are delivered faster, more sustainably, and at less cost.

I joined Nodes & Links in January 2022 and worked on their in-house B2B SaaS software product, where I:

  • Interviewed industry Risk experts to elicit existing product shortcomings and devised appropriate strategies for task prioritisation with a Product Owner and the Head of Product.
  • Gathered requirements for data-heavy screens and made suggestions for improvement, as well as implemented wireframes and designs according to my user research.
  • Created high fidelity, visually compelling and polished designs using Figma to present ideas and user flows.
  • Created rapid prototypes to ensure ideas are iterated through quickly, whilst maintaining a clear focus on channelling effective ideas in line with task story pointing and sprint deadlines.
  • Designed a suite of components accompanied with behaviours and interactions in the form of a Design System, which was constantly maintained and integrated with Storybook.
  • Worked across: Quantitative Schedule Risk Analysis (QSRA), Risk Drivers, Milestone Trend Analysis, Multi-Factor Authentication and Resources.
  • ...and more!

Work examples

Design System and Component Library

I created a mixture of single and multi-variant components, each with annotations that were presented to the development team instructing them on the intended use cases. To iron out any ambiguities, I also recorded short videos explaining the components in silo and in situ within the wider context of a product screen.

I worked with the development team to help integrate Storybook and Chromatic into our day-to-day workflows, which made it easier to implement component iterations seamlessly.

As the Senior Product Designer, I reported directly into the Head of Product. The overall team was comprised of 15–20 Software Engineers as well as Data Scientists, distributed across two squads. A Product Manager managed a squad each. As a designer, I worked directly with a Product Manager and Software Engineers, in an Agile fashion. We had stand-ups to times per week, where each sprint lasted for two weeks, top and tailed with sprint grooming and retrospectives.

Process and methodology

My role at Nodes & Links was largely around designing visually appealing screens for Project Risk, as well as Risk Drivers (what is the reason for this risk occurring), Milestone Trend Analysis (plotting project milestones and activities on a grid), Multi-Factor Authentication and Resources (project resources: equipment such as cranes, JCBs and their usage as well as material procurement such as cement).

Around Risk, I came up with design ideas for how to improve the existing Risk Analysis screens, and redesigned the Risk Table, Risk Register and Risk Template screens.

UX design

On the UX research side, this involved scheduling meetings with existing clients of the company, where I understood what they liked and found useful as well as what their pain points were. I also consulted industry risk experts to ascertain their objective views on the existing Risk pages, which qualitatively and quantitatively drove part of the project redesign.

UI design

Whilst I am unable to show final screen designs, I can paint a picture for you: Nodes & Links had no Product Designers when myself and the junior designer joined the company. I was tasked with designing the more complex screens, where I established foundations such as: working to an 8px grid, consistent iconography, a reusable page template that was intended to satisy the bulk of the pages, and just generally tidying up, streamlining and making the product look more sparkly.
